A Trip to the Countryside & Howth Cliffs

May 6, 2024 ~ Monday

Hooray for the first day of the trip! We went to Howth Cliffs straight from the Dublin airport to fight our looming jet lag. The cliffs were absolutely beautiful, right on the sea and surrounded by blooming yellow flowers. The steep stone steps led down to an incredible view.

After exploring Howth and eating some lovely seafood at Crabby Jo's, we went back to Dublin and checked into our lodging for the trip. From there we enjoyed a dinner at the oldest pub in Ireland, the Brazen Head!

Exploring Dublin: A City of History and Culture

May 7, 2024 ~ Tuesday

On this Tuesday, we had our first day of orientation for the CEA Capa program and went on a historic bus tour of Dublin! Our tour guide showed us many of the famous battle locations while telling us about the bigger parts of Irish history. We learned more about the Great Hunger of Ireland and how that led to Irish emigration across the world.

Some of the locations we went to on this tour included the People's Gardens, Church of the Sacred Heart which included a dedication to the buried rebels, the Jeanie Johnston Famine Ship & surrounding statues, and the Christchurch Cathedral.

First Day of Class!

SI 339 began today! We had our first class and created our website detailing our study abroad trip to Dublin, which you're on right now (inception...). After playing around with styling, adding some pictures, finding Irish fonts, and creating a blog, we wrapped up class for the day.

From there, we had the rest of the day to explore Dublin so we went to a charity shop and then explored Grafton Street! Guest Lecture with Dr David Williams: Games, Engagement, and Accessibility

May 9, 2024 ~ Thursday

In class today, we learned about flexbox, transitions, media queries, and responsive design. To apply these concepts, we played around with our image grids and adapted our webpages to be accessible and viewable on both mobile and desktop views. After class, we had a lunch break. After eating lunch back at my room, I came back to Griffith College campus and decided to try a coffee and a pastry from Noshington Cafe, the cafe down the street from our college. It was amazing and I will definitely be going back!

After we came back to campus, it was time for our guest lecture from Dr. David Williams and his wife, Anna Leary. I found this lecture to be very interesting and engaging, as it covered the topics of accessibility and gameification within the realm of web design. Dr. Williams talked about the theory behind gameification and Anna Leary showed us some practical examples of web design by detailing how she had to overhaul the Dublin Business School's Library website. Once class was over, we decided to grab dinner together in the Grafton Street neighborhood. We wanted to go to a really popular and well-reviewed taco restaurant called Masa, but it was fully booked so we agreed to make reservations and come another time. From there, we went to The Ramen Bar, which had great food and even better ambiance.

Visit to the EPIC Emigration Museum

First Friday in Dublin! In class today we talked about responsive web design and accessibility in navigation. We updated our navbars to display the current page more distinctly and made sure all of our links were active and working. After class, I went to the Liberty Market and browsed the variety of merchandise they had at the market. It was only a few blocks away from our lodging! Once we ended our museum visit, Lauren and I walked around the north side of the river, as we hadn't explored that area a lot. We ended up trying, (and LOVING) Irish Coffee at Vice Coffee, and we both also indulged in baked goods to satisfy our sugar cravings. Then we went to The Winding Stair, an adorable bookstore along the river where I got some postcards to send to my family. There were ebautiful watercolor renditions of Dublin sites, and I got one of St. Patrick's Park, since it is the park that is so close to our lodging. I'm excited to celebrate my first Friday night in Dublin and reflect on the first wonderful week!

Trip to Glendalough & Kilkenny

May 11, 2024 ~ Saturday

Today our whole group went on a day trip to Glendalough & Kilkenny, two beautiful areas of Ireland! At Glendalough, we explored the monastic ruins that exist there and learned about the history of St. Kevin's community. Then, we ventured down the trail surrounding the Wicklow Mountains to the lower & upper lakes, the former of which can be seen below. They were absolutely breath-taking, and had such picturesque views I almost couldn't believe they were real. I took a lot of pictures of the views, which exist on my Glendalough page. It was beautiful and sunny and there were even people swimming in the lakes! By the upper lake there was a tree that had a limb that was bent perfectly in the shape of a hammock, and there was someone relaxing and reading a book in it. Overall, Glendalough lived up to its Irish name of being the "Glen Between Lakes", and was an incredibly green and serene area.

From there, we went to Kilkenny to explore Kilkenny Castle and the surrounding town. More pictures from this trip can be found at my Kilkenny page. The castle itself was grand from the outside, but the restoration of the inside was the most amazing part. There were 20 different rooms and areas to explore within the castle, each restored to how it would've been when the castle was operational. My favorite room was the Picture Gallery Wing, a room filled with beautiful paintings that had dark, immersive lighting similar to what I've seen of Trinity College's Long Room. The green surrounding the castle was filled with people enjoying the sun and picnic-ing. A shopkeeper in Kilkenny told us that it was a lot busier that day because Bruce Springsteen was having a concert in town that night!

After the day trip, Lauren and I took the bus for the first time and got dinner at Kari, one of the most highly rated restaurants in Dublin. The food was absolutely incredible! It is an Indian restaurant, so I got the lamb biryani, and Lauren got chicken makhani. We both cleaned our plates in a matter of minutes, and the chef was so impressed with our speed and enjoyment that he brought us free dessert! The biryani was dotted with pomegranate seeds and spiced to perfection. I will definitely remember this meal as one of my favorites in Dublin, it was some of the best food I've had in a really long time!

A Tour of Belfast & Northern Ireland

May 12, 2024 ~ Sunday

On our free day, Sophie, Sarah, Lauren, Anastasia, and I decided to book a bus tour to Belfast & Northern Ireland to explore the northern part of the island! The tour left at 6am, and first took us to Belfast for a black cab tour of Belfast's political history (the dynamic of which is still a huge part of the city today). I'd never known about the extent of separation between the Unionist and Nationalist areas of Ireland, and it was incredibly interesting to learn about how their beliefs connect to other areas in world politics as well. Since we were in Northern Ireland, we were officially in the United Kingdom, which was something I hadn't quite wrapped my head around yet.

From the black cab tour, we went to Dunluce Castle, and then went to the Giant's Causeway, where we wrapped up the tour. Dunluce Castle was absolutely beautiful, as it lays right on the cliffs of Northern Ireland. The view was breathtaking, and the weather held out so we got to experience the castle to the fullest extent. Anastasia and I were able to go up inside of the round tower, which made the view even better. The Giant's Causeway was something I was incredibly excited to see, as my dad used to read me folk tales about it as a kid. Hearing the bus tour guide recap the folklore behind it's creation reminded me of my childhood, and it definitely exceeded expectations. The causeway itself is incredibly beautiful, and the color of the stones contrasted with the sea in a stunning manner. The hike down to the Causeway took us down the Shepherd's Steps, which were a steep set of stairs followed by a short trail down to the Causeway, complete with incredible scenery along the way. After our nature exploration, we decided to follow our tour guide's recommendation and get lunch at The Nook, 1 of 2 small restaurants at the Causeway. Our tour guide said that the Irish Stew at the Nook would be his death row meal should capital punishment ever return to Ireland, and so naturally, we all had to try it. It did not disappoint, and after our long day, it felt like one of the most satisfying meals I'd had in a long time. I've written more about this trip on my Belfast page.

Visit to the National Disability Authority of Ireland

May 13, 2024 ~ Monday

Today we finally got to experience some true Irish weather! It was rainy and a bit bleary for most of the day, but it got sunnier towards the end of the night. In class we reflected on our weekend by developing our Glendalough and Kilkenny pages, and reviewed the content that we've covered in the course thusfar. After class, I ate lunch at Little Bird Cafe and worked on my website for a while. Then, I headed back to Griffith College to meet up with the students to head to the National Disability Authority of Ireland.

There, Donal Rice gave a talk on how the Civil Rights movement was the precursor to the Accessibility movement, and in turn, the Web Accessibility movement. It was amazing to see how the history of advocacy and disability awareness connect, and to see how dynamic of a field it still is today. We compared European Union and United States approaches to disability policy, and discussed whose approach might be better. After the talk, Lauren and I explored Jam Art Factory near our lodging and I purchased a beautiful print of Dublin to put in my room in Ann Arbor. Afterwards, Anastasia and I went grocery shopping again and made dinner in our room. We made some delicious pasta and then headed out to meet Sophie, Sarah, and Lauren for a pint at the Lord Edward, which was supposed to have the best Guinness in town, according to our first tour guide. We tried it, and out of the three Guinnesses I've had, I can safely say it is the best one yet!

Afternoon Trip to the National Museum of History: Archaeology

May 14, 2024 ~ Tuesday

Today, we took advantage of the good weather, (and our newly topped-up Leap Cards), and took the DART train to Bray, Ireland! Bray is a coastal town south of Dublin that is known for it's amazing eateries and beautiful hiking. Sophie, Sarah, and I decided to do the Bray Head Loop Trail , which is a 10km loop that goes through the mountains of Bray Head. Our hike started off on an interesting foot because the entry gate was shut, and stated that the Bray Head Cliff Walk was closed due to rockfalls. We opened the entry gate and decided to proceed with caution through the Bray Head Loop. We were rewarded with the incredible views pictured below, along with lots of fun animal sightings along the way! We saw cows grazing, a hawk sitting on a tree, many starlings, and some fun dogs. The starlings were also present at our lunch restaurant, The Martello, and pounced on our food the second we got up from the table. Our waitress said they are omnipresent, and in her words, "They can't do anything about, even though they reign all over a woman, because they're endangered!" I can safely say that the hike was one of the prettiest hikes I've ever been on! The views from the top allowed us to see a 360 degree view of Bray Head and the surrounding town. The hills were coated with yellow flowers, and the rocky mountains were made from granite formations. Even the train ride there was along the water most of the time, so it was a day filled with breath-taking scenery.

Company Visit at Microsoft Ireland: EMEA Headquarters

May 15, 2024 ~ Wednesday

In class today, we reflected on our favorite things about Ireland so far, and then spent some time beefing up the content of our websites. After class, my mom's friend from college, Archana, brought me chicken biryani, so I got to have a taste of home for lunch! We relaxed for a bit during lunch and then went to the St. Stephen's Green station to take the LUAS tram to the Microsoft Ireland HQ. The tram moved incredibly slow, but it was nice to see the different parts of Dublin further from when we live. At Microsoft, we got a tour of their office, which I've included some pictures from below. It was a beautiful (and MASSIVE) office, with lots of natural light from the circular ceiling skylights. After we got a tour of their various amenities such as a sleeping area, a yoga room, and a roof patio lounge, we went into the conference room to hear a presentation about AI. Victor and Linda, our tour guides, talked about Microsoft's history and the new AI projects they are investing in, such as Sora and Copilot. I'd never known how connected Microsoft and OpenAI are, so it was interesting to hear about the various developments in AI and how that connects to Microsoft's role as a company. We learned about the details of the different models that OpenAI has created, such as GPT4, GPT4V, DALL-E, and Sora. Overall, I learned a lot more about AI and the developments that the future holds, and am excited to see where this technology goes in the next few years!

After the Microsoft visit, Lauren, Sam, Anastasia and I took the LUAS back home, after buying a new ticket because I'd somehow lost my Leap Card during the visit. 3 stops in, the power failed on the entire green line, so we had to get off and find another way home. We decided to split a taxi, and agreed to celebrate our minor inconvenience by enjoying the student meal deal at John's Bar & Haberdashery again, which is a burger, fries, and a pint for 13 Euros. It was a great way to destress from the day! After that, Lauren and I realized that TooGoodToGo, a food waste prevention app, works really well in Dublin. Any extra food that restaurants or cafes have after the day ends can be put onto the app, and people can purchase it for discounted prices. Lauren and I decided to split a 6 pack of donuts from the Off Beat Donut Company, which were absolutely incredible. I walked to the Trinity College area to pick up the donuts, and it was bustling with students getting ready for a night out. Once I retrieved the donuts, Sam, Ben, Lauren, Anastasia, Mari, and I went to Street 66, where we played a game called Taco Cat Goat Cheese Pizza, and hung out for a few hours.
