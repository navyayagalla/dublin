A Trip to the Countryside & Howth Cliffs

May 6, 2024 ~ Monday

Hooray for the first day of the trip! We went to Howth Cliffs straight from the Dublin airport to fight our looming jet lag. The cliffs were absolutely beautiful, right on the sea and surrounded by blooming yellow flowers. The steep stone steps led down to an incredible view.


After exploring Howth and eating some lovely seafood at Crabby Jo's, we went back to Dublin and checked into our lodging for the trip. From there we enjoyed a dinner at the oldest pub in Ireland, the Brazen Head!

Exploring Dublin: A City of History and Culture

May 7, 2024 ~ Tuesday

On this Tuesday, we had our first day of orientation for the CEA Capa program and went on a historic bus tour of Dublin! Our tour guide showed us many of the famous battle locations while telling us about the bigger parts of Irish history. We learned more about the Great Hunger of Ireland and how that led to Irish emigration across the world.


Some of the locations we went to on this tour included the People's Gardens, Church of the Sacred Heart which included a dedication to the buried rebels, the Jeanie Johnston Famine Ship & surrounding statues, and the Christchurch Cathedral.

First Day of Class!

May 8, 2024 ~ Wednesday

SI 339 began today! We had our first class and created our website detailing our study abroad trip to Dublin, which you're on right now (inception...). After playing around with styling, adding some pictures, finding Irish fonts, and creating a blog, we wrapped up class for the day.


From there, we had the rest of the day to explore Dublin so we went to a charity shop and then explored Grafton Street! We grabbed some coffee and enjoyed spending time in nature at St. Stephen's Green. Finally, we went to John's Bar & Haberdashery to take advantage of their student burger meal deal.

Guest Lecture with Dr David Williams: Games, Engagement, and Accessibility

May 9, 2024 ~ Thursday

In class today, we learned about flexbox, transitions, media queries, and responsive design. To apply these concepts, we played around with our image grids and adapted our webpages to be accessible and viewable on both mobile and desktop views. After class, we had a lunch break. After eating lunch back at my room, I came back to Griffith College campus and decided to try a coffee and a pastry from Noshington Cafe, the cafe down the street from our college. It was amazing and I will definitely be going back!


After we came back to campus, it was time for our guest lecture from Dr. David Williams and his wife, Anna Leary. I found this lecture to be very interesting and engaging, as it covered the topics of accessibility and gameification within the realm of web design. Dr. Williams talked about the theory behind gameification and Anna Leary showed us some practical examples of web design by detailing how she had to overhaul the Dublin Business School's Library website. Once class was over, we decided to grab dinner together in the Grafton Street neighborhood. We wanted to go to a really popular and well-reviewed taco restaurant called Masa, but it was fully booked so we agreed to make reservations and come another time. From there, we went to The Ramen Bar, which had great food and even better ambiance.

Visit to the EPIC Emigration Museum

May 10, 2024 ~ Friday

First Friday in Dublin! In class today we talked about responsive web design and accessibility in navigation. We updated our navbars to display the current page more distinctly and made sure all of our links were active and working. After class, I went to the Liberty Market and browsed the variety of merchandise they had at the market. It was only a few blocks away from our lodging! I bought a Turkish bracelet before going home to eat lunch.


After lunch, I walked to the EPIC Emigration Museum for our class visit. Almost the whole walk was along the water, so it was a beautiful view to take in! It was beautifully sunny today so I wanted to take advantage of the nice weather. The emigration museum was incredibly detailed and informative, and I felt like I learned alot about the places that Irish people went to after the famine, and the lives they formed once they got there. Lauren and I struck up a great conversation with one of the museum docents about local's recommendations for Dublin, Maymesters, and what life at University College Dublin is like for her.


Once we ended our museum visit, Lauren and I walked around the north side of the river, as we hadn't explored that area a lot. We ended up trying, (and LOVING) Irish Coffee at Vice Coffee, and we both also indulged in baked goods to satisfy our sugar cravings. Then we went to The Winding Stair, an adorable bookstore along the river where I got some postcards to send to my family. There were ebautiful watercolor renditions of Dublin sites, and I got one of St. Patrick's Park, since it is the park that is so close to our lodging. I'm excited to celebrate my first Friday night in Dublin and reflect on the first wonderful week!
