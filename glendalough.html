Glendalough

About Glendalough


Glendalough is located in Wicklow County, Ireland, and it's name directly translates to glen of the 2 lakes. Like the name suggests, it is situated in a valley between 2 beautiful lakes, and contains the remains of the monastic city founded by St. Kevin. We visited the ruins of the city and got to explore the round tower, St. Kevin's house, and what is left of the cathedral. The site is also a graveyard, and acts as a burial site for many families. A surrounding field where sheep graze brings peace to the site.


A short walk down the path surrounding the Wicklow Mountains will bring you to the lower lake, which boasts spectacular views of the valley. Walk a little further and you will reach the upper lake, which is the site of the most picturesque views. A massive lake allows for swimming and enjoyment of nature, with a picnic green and other areas of relaxation around it. From here you can truly see how the lake is shaped by the mountains in the area.
